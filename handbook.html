CS Handbook Chrisland University

Complete guide to academic policies, regulations, and student life.

Our Vision

To be the leading Department in Research and Teaching towards the economic and developmental growth of the nation through ICT.

Our Mission

  • Stimulate sustained interest and enthusiasm in computer science.
  • Train students with necessary computing knowledge and skills for effective service.
  • Provide solid background for postgraduate studies and research.
  • Conduct research to advance computer science and integrate into other disciplines.
Read more about our philosophy

Admission Requirements

  • O' Level: Five credits including English, Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry + any other science.
  • UTME Subjects: English, Mathematics, Physics, and one of Chemistry/Biology.
  • Direct Entry: ND/NCE in relevant fields with O' Level requirements.

Grading System

  • 70-100% = A (5 pts) - First Class
  • 60-69% = B (4 pts) - Second Class Upper
  • 50-59% = C (3 pts) - Second Class Lower
  • 45-49% = D (2 pts) - Third Class
  • 40-44% = E (1 pt) - Pass
  • Below 40% = F (0 pts) - Fail

Key Regulations

  • Minimum workload: 15 units, maximum: 24 units per semester.
  • GPA < 1.50 leads to probation; CGPA < 1.50 for two consecutive semesters → withdrawal.
  • 75% lecture attendance mandatory to sit for exams.

Dress Code

Female: Skirt below knees, no transparent/ tight clothes, no excessive makeup, moderate jewellery, hair well-groomed. Tattoos prohibited.

Male: Trousers (not jeans), shirt and tie, no earrings/necklaces, hair short, clean-shaven.

Health Services

  • All students must register with the University Health Centre.
  • Medical reports from outside must be endorsed by the Director.
  • In case of illness during exams, report immediately to the Dean.

Library Rules

  • Silence must be observed; phones on silent.
  • Books must be checked out; no removal without authorization.
  • Bags inspected on exit.
  • Library ID card required for entry.

Academic Calendar 2025/2026

  • Exams: Dec 5 – Dec 20
  • Second Semester: Jan 15 – April 30
  • Exams: April 20 – April 30

Registration Procedures

  1. Clearance by Admissions Officer and collection of forms.
  2. Payment of fees and Bursary clearance.
  3. Allocation of hostel (if applicable).
  4. Faculty Office clearance.
  5. Course registration in consultation with HOD.
  6. Submit completed forms to Academic Office.
  7. Register at Health Centre.

Late registration attracts a penalty of ₦2,500 per week. No registration after matriculation.

Full Philosophy & Mission

University Philosophy: To provide a first-class academic and research environment where the needs of the society and mankind are paramount. It aims to create an atmosphere of academic freedom, nurture research culture, provide liberal education, build knowledge creators, and foster community service.

Departmental Vision (detailed): To be the leading Department in Research and Teaching towards the economic and developmental growth of the nation through ICT.

Departmental Mission (detailed): Stimulate sustained interest in computer science; train students with necessary computing knowledge and skills; provide solid background for postgraduate studies; conduct research to advance the state of the art.

Core Values: Innovation, Integrity, Excellence, Leadership.
